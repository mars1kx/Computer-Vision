Your final assignment for this course is a team project. Groups of 2–4 students will build a working Computer Vision system that solves a real visual problem — from detecting empty shelves to counting people, classifying fruit, or anything you propose yourself.

The system must follow a full pipeline: enhance → segment → clean → detect → decide. No theory-only work — the system must run on real images and produce an automatic result.

At the end of the semester you will present and defend your project as a team. The project score is shared — your team then decides how to split the points among yourselves.

Pick your theme, form your team, and register it with the teacher. Each theme can only be taken by one team.
